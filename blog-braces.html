What to Expect During Braces Treatment

Getting braces is one of the most effective ways to achieve a straighter, healthier smile. But for many patients — especially first-timers — the process can feel uncertain. What will it feel like? How long will it take? What can you eat? This guide walks you through every stage so you know exactly what to expect.

Initial Consultation

Your journey begins with a consultation where your orthodontist examines your teeth, jaw, and bite. They'll take X-rays, photos, and sometimes a digital or plaster impression of your teeth. This data helps them map out a treatment plan tailored to your specific alignment needs.

At Smile Concept, we make sure you fully understand the plan before anything is attached — including the expected duration, the type of braces recommended, and an honest picture of the result.

Fitting Day

Once your plan is confirmed, you'll come in for the fitting appointment. Your orthodontist will clean and dry your teeth, apply a bonding adhesive, and attach the brackets. The archwire is then threaded through and secured with small elastic bands. The whole process typically takes 1–2 hours.

You may feel pressure during fitting, but it should not be painful. The first 3–5 days after fitting, however, is when most patients experience soreness as the teeth begin to shift.

The First Few Weeks

Discomfort in the initial weeks is normal and manageable. Here's what helps:

  • Stick to soft foods — rice, soup, yoghurt, mashed potatoes
  • Use orthodontic wax on any brackets that irritate your cheeks
  • Rinse with warm salt water to soothe soreness
  • Take over-the-counter pain relief if needed

Adjustment Appointments

Every 4–8 weeks, you'll return for an adjustment. Your orthodontist will change the archwire or tighten it to continue guiding tooth movement. Each adjustment may bring a day or two of mild soreness — this is a sign that the treatment is working.

These appointments are also a chance to ask questions, check your oral hygiene, and monitor progress.

Foods to Avoid

Certain foods can damage brackets or get stuck in the wires. Avoid:

  • Hard foods — nuts, ice, hard candy, raw carrots
  • Sticky foods — caramel, chewing gum, dried fruit
  • Foods you bite directly — apples, corn on the cob (cut them up instead)

Oral Hygiene with Braces

Keeping your teeth clean with braces requires more care than usual. Plaque can accumulate around brackets and wires, increasing the risk of cavities and gum issues. Brush after every meal using a soft-bristle brush, and use an interdental brush or floss threader to clean between teeth and under the wire.

The Final Result

Most braces treatments take between 12 and 24 months, depending on the complexity of alignment needed. Once your orthodontist is satisfied with the positioning, the braces come off — a moment most patients describe as deeply satisfying.

After removal, you'll be fitted with a retainer to hold your teeth in their new position. Wearing your retainer as prescribed is essential — without it, teeth can gradually shift back.
